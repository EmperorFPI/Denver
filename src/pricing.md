Most notary pricing pages are deliberately vague, because vagueness lets the number grow at your kitchen table. Nevada law actually makes honest pricing easy — the state caps the core fee, requires disclosure of everything else, and requires an itemized written record. So here is everything, itemized.

## The parts of the price

<div class="table-scroll">

| Component | Amount | Who sets it |
|---|---|---|
| Notarial act, in person | **$15 per signature** (maximum) | Nevada law |
| Notarial act, remote online (RON) | **$25 per act** (maximum) | Nevada law |
| Travel fee | Varies by distance, time of day | The notary — must be disclosed upfront |
| After-hours / urgent premium | Disclosed at quote, when it applies | The notary — must be disclosed upfront |
| Printing / package handling (loan signings) | Included in flat package price | The notary — itemized |

</div>

## What real visits total

- **Single document, valley address, business hours:** typically **$45–$65** all-in
- **Multiple documents or signers, one visit:** **$60–$95** — each notarized signature is its own capped act, the travel is shared
- **Hospital or facility visit:** usually the standard range; after-hours urgency adds a disclosed premium
- **Loan signing package:** flat quote, commonly **$125–$200** depending on package size and print requirements
- **RON session, single document:** usually **under a mobile visit** — the $25 act plus platform fees, no travel

Far-suburb addresses, 6 AM or 9 PM slots, and wait-time-heavy appointments cost more, and the quote will say so *before* you book — never after.

## The rule that protects you

Nevada requires a notary to inform you of **all** charges before performing the act and to provide a written, itemized record of the fees. A notary who springs charges at the table isn't just being slippery — they're violating the terms of their commission. Our structure removes the temptation entirely: one quoted number, payment processed at booking, and an appointment that involves signatures instead of negotiations.
