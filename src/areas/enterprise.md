Enterprise is the valley's growth engine on the southwest side — Mountain's Edge, Southern Highlands, Rhodes Ranch, Silverado Ranch, and the new rooftops still going up toward the 215 and St. Rose Parkway. It's young, it commutes, and it runs on two clocks: closing dates on the new builds, and refinances and family paperwork on the ones that closed a few years ago. A notary service here has to be good at both, and available after work.

## What Enterprise actually calls us for

**New-build purchase closings.** The southwest growth corridor generates full buyer signing packages on builder timelines. We run these as [flat-price loan signings](/services/loan-signing/): every initial and date where the lender wants it, notarized acts sealed, package returned same-day per your title company's instructions.

**Refinance and HELOC signings.** As Enterprise homeowners build equity, home-equity work follows — often borrower-arranged, which means you're the one finding the notary. Evening kitchen-table appointments in Mountain's Edge, Southern Highlands, and Rhodes Ranch are the standard play.

**Family paperwork.** Minor travel consent forms lead the category (notarized consent from the non-traveling parent — airlines really do ask), followed by school guardianship affidavits, sports and activity waivers, and passport-adjacent documents. This is one of the youngest, most family-dense parts of the valley, and it shows in the calls.

**Estate starts, not just estate refreshes.** Younger homeowners here are often signing their *first* wills, trusts, and POAs — frequently with witnesses required beyond the notary, which we flag at booking rather than discovering at the table.

## Coverage and timing

All of Enterprise — Mountain's Edge, Southern Highlands, Rhodes Ranch, Silverado Ranch, and the newer tracts toward St. Rose Parkway — plus fast reaches into Spring Valley, Henderson, and the southwest edge of the Strip corridor. Same-day for most daytime requests; evening slots book quickly, so call earlier in the day when you can. Nevada caps the notarial act at $15 per signature everywhere — the travel component is quoted upfront, and payment happens at booking so the appointment is just signatures.
