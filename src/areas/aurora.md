Aurora generates more urgent notary calls per capita than almost anywhere else we serve, and the map explains why: the Anschutz Medical Campus concentrates thousands of hospital beds in one square mile, Buckley Space Force Base cycles families through deployments, and one of Colorado's most international communities constantly needs documents that work across borders.

## What Aurora actually calls us for

**Hospital bedside work at Anschutz.** UCHealth University of Colorado Hospital, Children's Hospital Colorado, and the VA medical center sit side by side, and pre-surgery powers of attorney and medical directives are our single most common Aurora request. These visits succeed on preparation — the signer must be alert and have ID available — so we screen carefully by phone before dispatching. [How hospital visits work →](/services/hospital-jail-notary/)

**Military and deployment paperwork.** POAs, vehicle title transfers before a PCS move, and consent forms for a parent traveling solo with kids. We'll say the honest thing first: base legal offices notarize for free if you can get an appointment. When you can't — or when it's the spouse at home handling everything at 7 PM on a Sunday — that's us.

**International and apostille documents.** Aurora residents regularly need single-status affidavits, POAs for property abroad, and school records heading overseas. The notarization has to be executed correctly for the Colorado Secretary of State to apostille it, and that's the part [we control](/services/apostille/).

**Everything ordinary, too.** Title transfers on East Colfax car deals, affidavits for Arapahoe and Adams County court filings (Aurora famously straddles three counties), and refinance signings from Del Mar to Saddle Rock.

## Coverage and timing

All of Aurora, all three counties of it — from the Stanley Marketplace end of Stapleton-adjacent north Aurora down through the DTC-side neighborhoods and out to Southlands. Same-day service is standard for requests placed before mid-afternoon; the far southeast carries a small, disclosed travel adjustment. Colorado's $15-per-act cap applies everywhere, always.
