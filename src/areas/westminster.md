Westminster sits in the seam of the north metro — Denver-based notaries consider it far, Boulder-based ones consider it Denver's problem, and 115,000 people live in the seam. The US 36 corridor packs in business parks, the city straddles two counties, and a substantial senior population lives in communities from 72nd Avenue up to Legacy Ridge.

## What Westminster actually calls us for

**Business documents on the corridor.** The office parks between Church Ranch and Interlocken's edge generate commercial leases, corporate resolutions, vendor agreements, and I-9-adjacent paperwork needing notarized signatures. We come to the conference room; multiple signers in one visit is normal, and each notarized signature is its own $15-capped act — quoted as one clean number.

**Senior community appointments.** Powers of attorney, medical directives, and estate updates at Westminster's assisted and independent living communities. As with our [hospital and facility work](/services/hospital-jail-notary/), the signer's awareness at signing is the legal requirement, and we prepare families for that honestly rather than dispatching blind.

**Two-county household paperwork.** Adams County on one side of the line, Jefferson on the other — affidavits, name changes, and probate documents flow to both courthouses from Westminster addresses. The notarization is identical either way; knowing which county your filing belongs to is the part worth double-checking.

**The north-metro standard mix.** Refis and [HELOCs](/services/loan-signing/) in the established neighborhoods off Sheridan and Federal, title transfers, and travel consent forms for DIA-bound families.

## Coverage and timing

All of Westminster — 72nd Avenue to 144th, Federal to Standley Lake — with quick reaches into Broomfield, Thornton, Northglenn, and Arvada. Same-day service is standard for calls before mid-afternoon. Full price quoted before booking: Colorado's capped $15 per notarial act plus the disclosed travel fee, paid at booking, never at the table.
