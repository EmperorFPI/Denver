Centennial is quietly one of the most document-heavy cities in Colorado: high homeownership, high home values, an older-skewing professional population, and the Denver Tech Center pressing against its northern border. Translation — trusts, HELOCs, and corporate paperwork, in volume.

## What Centennial actually calls us for

**Trust and estate packages.** This is the signature Centennial appointment: a living trust with its pour-over will, durable and medical POAs, and a deed moving the house into the trust — executed in one sitting at the dining room table. The thing that derails these isn't the notary; it's **witness requirements** on certain documents, which we solve at booking, not at the table. Attorney-prepared packages are welcome; we coordinate with your attorney's instructions and stay firmly in the notary lane.

**HELOC and refinance signings.** Centennial equity is substantial and [home-equity signings](/services/loan-signing/) — frequently borrower-arranged, meaning you're the one who has to find the notary — are steady evening work from Willow Creek to Piney Creek. Flat package price, same-day document return.

**DTC-adjacent business documents.** Conference-room notarizations along the Arapahoe Road and I-25 edge: resolutions, commercial leases, franchise documents, relocation paperwork. Multiple signers in one visit is the norm.

**Family logistics.** Cherry Creek Schools generates its own paper economy — guardianship affidavits, travel consent forms, activity waivers — and a ten-minute evening visit handles most of it.

## Coverage and timing

All of Centennial, from the DTC edge east through Piney Creek and Antelope, with quick reaches into Greenwood Village, Englewood, Littleton, and Highlands Ranch. Same-day for most requests placed before mid-afternoon; evening trust signings book ahead, so give those a day or two when you can. Price quoted in full before booking, payment processed at booking — the appointment itself is just pens and seals.
