Arvada runs on two clocks. Northwest Arvada — Candelas, Leyden Rock, the streets still getting sod — runs on closing dates. The established grid between Olde Town and Sheridan runs on refinances, estate updates, and the paperwork of families deep in the school years. A notary service here has to be good at both, and available after work, because Arvada commutes.

## What Arvada actually calls us for

**New-build purchase closings.** The northwest growth corridor generates full buyer signing packages on builder timelines that don't slip politely. We run these as [flat-price loan signings](/services/loan-signing/): every initial and date where the lender wants it, notarized acts sealed, package returned same-day per your title company's instructions.

**Refinance and HELOC signings in the established grid.** Decades of equity in the ranches and split-levels off Wadsworth and 64th means steady home-equity work — often borrower-arranged, which means you're the one finding the notary. Evening kitchen-table appointments are the standard play.

**Family paperwork.** Minor travel consent forms lead the category (notarized consent from the non-traveling parent — airlines really do ask), followed by school guardianship affidavits, sports and activity waivers, and passport-adjacent documents.

**Estate refresh appointments.** Long-time Arvada homeowners updating wills, adding transfer-on-death deeds, and signing POAs — frequently with witnesses required beyond the notary, which we flag at booking rather than discovering at the table.

## Coverage and timing

All of Arvada, from the Olde Town core to Candelas at the far northwest, plus fast reaches into Wheat Ridge, Westminster, and Golden. Same-day for most daytime requests; evening slots book quickly, so call earlier in the day when you can. Colorado caps the notarial act at $15 per document everywhere — the travel component is quoted upfront, and payment happens at booking so the appointment is just signatures.
