Remote online notarization is the single biggest change to notary law in a century, and Colorado adopted it properly: a live, recorded audio-video session with a commissioned Colorado notary, layered identity verification, and a digital seal that most banks, title companies, and agencies now accept.

## What RON actually is

You upload your document, verify your identity through an ID scan plus a knowledge-based quiz, then join a live video session with the notary. You sign electronically on camera; the notary applies an electronic seal and digital certificate; the session recording is retained under Colorado's rules. You get the completed, tamper-evident document back the same day — usually within the hour.

It is **not** "sign a PDF and email it." A RON notarization has more identity verification than most in-person notarizations, which is exactly why states allowed it.

## When RON is the right tool

- You're **out of state or overseas** with Colorado paperwork that can't wait for the mail
- You need a notarization **tonight** and a physical visit doesn't make sense
- The signer is **mobility-limited** but comfortable on a video call
- Multiple signers are in **different cities** and one document needs everyone
- You simply don't want a stranger in your living room — fair enough

## When RON is the wrong tool

We'd rather lose a booking than have your document bounce. RON is the wrong choice when the receiving party requires wet ink (some county recorders, some lenders, many foreign consulates), when the document is a will or estate instrument that your attorney wants executed in person, or when the signer can't manage the technology under time pressure. In each of those cases, an [in-person mobile visit](/services/mobile-notary/) is the honest recommendation, and we'll make it.

## Pricing and turnaround

Colorado caps the RON notarial act at **$25 per act**. Platform and session fees are itemized separately and quoted before you book — total cost for a typical single-document RON session is usually **less than a mobile visit**, because nobody's driving anywhere. Most sessions are completed same-day, and rush sessions can often start within the hour during business hours.
