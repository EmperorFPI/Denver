Most people discover they need a notary at the worst possible moment: the POA has to be signed before Dad's surgery Thursday, the title buyer is waiting, the bank's one notary is "at lunch" indefinitely. Mobile notary service exists for exactly that moment — instead of you hunting for a notary, a commissioned notary drives to you.

## What a mobile notary actually does

A notary public is a state-commissioned impartial witness. At your appointment, the notary verifies each signer's identity against a valid government photo ID, confirms the signer is acting willingly and appears aware of what they're signing, witnesses the signature (or takes an acknowledgment of it), completes the notarial certificate, applies the official stamp, and records the act in a journal as Nevada law requires.

That's the whole job — and it's enough. The seal is what makes courts, county recorders, banks, and government agencies accept your document without you standing in front of them personally.

## When you'd call us

- **Powers of attorney and medical directives** — often urgent, often for a signer who can't travel
- **Real estate documents** — deeds, quitclaims, seller docs when only one side needs a notary
- **Vehicle and trailer titles** — including out-of-state titles that require notarized transfers
- **Affidavits and sworn statements** — court filings, name-change paperwork, custody documents
- **Business documents** — commercial leases, corporate resolutions, vendor agreements
- **School and travel consent forms** — minor travel consent is one of our most common calls

## What a notary can't do

Being straight about the limits saves everyone a wasted trip. A Nevada notary **cannot** draft or complete your document, give legal advice about what it means, notarize a document with blank spaces, notarize their own signature or a document they benefit from, or certify copies of vital records like birth certificates (those come from the state registrar). If what you actually need is legal advice, you need an attorney — and we'll tell you that on the phone rather than bill you to find out.

One more that surprises people: **don't sign in advance.** For most notarial acts, the signature must happen in the notary's presence. Bring the document complete but unsigned.

## Pricing and turnaround

Nevada caps the notarial act at **$15 per signature**. The travel fee is separate, disclosed upfront, and is most of what a mobile visit costs. Typical Las Vegas-valley appointments run **$45–$95 all-in**; after-hours, far-suburb, and multi-document appointments run higher, and you'll know the exact number before you book. Payment is processed before the appointment, so the visit itself is just signatures and a handshake.

Same-day service is the norm, not the exception. Call before mid-afternoon and we can usually reach you that day anywhere in the valley.
